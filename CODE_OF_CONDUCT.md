# Code of Conduct

Lux IDE uses a simple contribution standard: be direct, technical, and respectful. Strong opinions are welcome when they are backed by evidence and focused on improving the project.

## Expected Behavior

- Keep discussion about the code, product behavior, architecture, or documentation.
- Give actionable feedback with enough context for someone to respond.
- Assume contributors have different time zones, languages, and experience levels.
- Accept maintainer decisions after the tradeoffs are explained.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, personal attacks, or discriminatory language.
- Repeated off-topic arguments after maintainers ask to stop.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Spam, low-effort issue flooding, or intentionally misleading reports.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, lock, or delete comments; close issues or PRs; and restrict participation for behavior that harms the project.

Report serious conduct issues privately through the maintainer contact published for the project.
